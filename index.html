Week-by-week breakdown
Pre-phase Days 1–10 · Jun 15 – Jun 24
Architect preparation & champion identification
Architects complete foundational training before the 30-day plan begins. In parallel, product QA Managers identify and nominate champions based on defined criteria.
Architect training
  • Deep dive into GitHub Actions and workflow YAML structure
  • QA STLC phase mapping — unit, integration, regression, release gates
  • Runner types — hosted vs self-hosted, configuration and sizing
  • Workflow triggers, caching strategies, and artifact management
  • Hands-on creation of sample QA pipeline per STLC phase
Champion selection (parallel)
  • Architects define and share selection criteria with all QA Managers
  • Each product QA Manager nominates 1–2 champions from their team
  • Nominations reviewed and confirmed — target 30 champions total
  • Champion roster published and shared with all architects
  • Champions informed of plan, dates, and expectations
✓ Outcome: 4 trained architects + 30 identified champions ready to begin
Week 1 Days 11–17 · Jun 25 – Jul 1
Onboarding and foundations
All 30 champions come together for group sessions led by the 4 architects. Focus is on establishing a shared baseline — tools, goals, and ways of working.
Sessions and setup
  • Kickoff session — plan walkthrough, goals, and success criteria
  • realpage-powerup setup verification for all 30 champions
  • Claude access confirmed and local environment validated
  • Intro to AI agents — user story evaluation, test case generation, PR analyzer
  • Overview of GitHub workflow structure and QA STLC alignment
Focus areas introduced
  • Test case generation & STLC coverage — concepts and live demo
  • PR reviews & automation scripts — live walkthrough
  • GitHub workflows & runner strategy — architecture overview
  • Code validation, report creation & analysis — tooling intro
  • Q&A and feedback collection from all champions
✓ Outcome: All 30 champions onboarded, tools ready, baseline knowledge established
Week 2 Days 18–24 · Jul 2 – Jul 8
Hands-on training — product group sessions
Each architect takes their assigned ~4 champions through intensive, product-specific training. Sessions are practical — champions work on real tasks from their own products.
Training activities
  • Test case generation using Claude — writing, reviewing, and refining TCs
  • Automating PR pre-reviews with AI — hands-on PR walkthroughs
  • Building GitHub workflows for unit and integration phases
  • Code validation techniques — static analysis and reporting outputs
  • Architect-guided pair sessions on real sprint work
Cross-product activities
  • Mid-week cross-product sync — champions share early learnings
  • Best practice examples collected and shared in shared repo
  • Blocker resolution — architects address common challenges
  • Champions begin documenting their product-specific workflows
  • Peer review of each other's test cases and automation scripts
✓ Outcome: Champions hands-on with all 4 focus areas, initial workflows drafted
Week 3 Days 25–31 · Jul 9 – Jul 15
Deep dives and applied practice
Champions move from guided learning to applied practice. Architects shift to a coaching role — reviewing work, unblocking, and pushing champions toward independent execution.
Applied work
  • Champions complete end-to-end GitHub workflow for their product
  • Full STLC pipeline built — from unit tests to release gate
  • Automation scripts reviewed, refined, and committed to repo
  • Report creation — champions generate and present analysis outputs
  • Runner configuration completed — hosted or self-hosted as applicable
Collaboration and review
  • Cross-product showcase — each champion presents their workflow
  • Peer feedback sessions facilitated by architects
  • Edge case scenarios tested — failure handling, retries, alerts
  • Champions begin preparing training material for their own products
  • Progress check — architects assess readiness for certification
✓ Outcome: Full workflows operational, champions ready for independent execution
Week 4 Days 32–40 · Jul 16 – Jul 24
Independent practice and certification
Champions operate independently with light-touch architect oversight. The week culminates in certification sign-off and each champion running their first team training session.
Certification tasks
  • Independent delivery of a complete QA workflow with no architect guidance
  • Live demonstration of all 4 focus areas to their architect
  • Submit documented workflow and training material for sign-off
  • Architect assessment — quality, coverage, and knowledge check
  • Formal certification sign-off by assigned architect
Cascade to products
  • Each champion runs a training session with their own product team
  • Product teams onboarded to realpage-powerup and AI agents
  • Champion establishes themselves as go-to AI enablement contact
  • Feedback loop opened — champions report back to architects monthly
  • Continuous improvement cycle kicks off across all products
✓ Outcome: 30 certified champions — AI and GitHub workflow adoption cascading org-wide
